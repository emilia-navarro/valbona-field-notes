# Cultural Landscape Observations: Guesthouse Architecture in Valbona

## Purpose

This file records qualitative observations about built-form change in the Valbona Valley. It is intentionally written as cultural-landscape field notation rather than as a quantified land-use study.

## Working Observation

Traditional stone architecture and highland guesthouse forms are increasingly embedded within a tourism-oriented accommodation landscape. In many sections of the valley, older stonework, family compounds, and vernacular forms coexist with newer guesthouse expansions, timber-concrete hybrid buildings, and service infrastructure.

## Vernacular Architecture Notes

Observed or commonly reported features include:

- stone walls and masonry elements;
- pitched roofs adapted to mountain weather;
- family-run guesthouse compounds;
- reuse or adaptation of older structures for hospitality;
- visual contrast between lower-density vernacular buildings and newer accommodation-oriented construction.

These notes should be validated through dated photography, building-footprint comparison, interviews, or cadastral/permit data before being used as academic evidence.

## Tourism-Driven Spatial Change

A defensible formulation:

> The Valbona corridor shows visible signs of accommodation-led development, especially near road-accessible sections and guesthouse clusters. The scale and rate of this change require verification through longitudinal land-use analysis before quantitative claims are made.

## Suggested Research Method for Quantification

To convert this note into a measurable study:

1. Define the study corridor, for example Rragam–Valbonë central road corridor.
2. Collect satellite imagery or building-footprint layers for at least two dates, e.g. 2020 and 2026.
3. Classify structures by footprint, material proxy, or land-use class.
4. Validate ambiguous buildings with field photographs or local interviews.
5. Report uncertainty, classification limits, and seasonal visibility constraints.
6. Publish the dataset or analysis notebook alongside the conclusion.

## Socio-Economic Interpretation

The shift from pastoral and household-based land use toward hospitality-oriented space is plausible and visible in the tourism economy, but should be framed carefully. Use “observed”, “reported”, or “requires validation” unless backed by a formal dataset.
